Cooling Down The Xeon Phi SKU 31S1P

A few months ago, Dr Z who’s with the CAPSL team sent me a Xeon Phi co-processor:

This particular board, SKU 31S1P, is not usually installed inside desktop computers as it is meant to be passively cooled in HPC data center environment.

Airflow requirements for the passive cooling solutions are listed in the section 3.3.2 of the coprocessor datasheet:

In order to ensure adequate cooling of the SE10P/7120P/3120P 300W and 31S1P 270W SKUs with a 45oC inlet temperature, the system must be able to provide 33 ft3/ min of airflow to the card with 7.2 ft3/min on the secondary side and the remainder on the primary side. The total pressure drop (assuming a multi-card installation conforming to the PCI Express* mechanical specification) is 0.54 in H2O at this flow rate.

Tests

I tested the fan duct with 2 different 12VDC fans:

  • a YATE LOON D80SH-12, 12V, 0.18A 32ft3/m, 34 dBA ;
  • a Delta Electronics FFB0812EHE, 12V, 1.35A, 80.16ft3/m, 52.5 dBA

To test, I’m running the Embree high performance raytracing kernels examples on the Phi and monitoring temperature VS power.

D80SH-12

  • 67°C@103W
  • 73°C@121W
  • 86°C@168W

According to Intel that’s too much:

Delta Electronics FFB0812EHE

  • 39°C@93W
  • 42°C@112W
  • 48°C@156W

This is good but …

it’s extremely noisy (rated 52.5 dBA …) so that I simply can’t work near the box and must unplug/remove the Phi from the computer when I’m not using it. Not a big problem as it’s just a hobby.
